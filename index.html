Immunibasin - A vaccine storage device created for Developing Countries

Over the first term, we were paired up with 4 other Biomedical Engineering Students to tackle global health in a mechanical way.

We noticed that vaccine potability was a big issue within the developing world. Vaccines being transferred to remote locations are used in hopes to extend the lives of many. However, due to the equatorial climate present in those developing countries, the vaccines expire or spoil before they reach the location of need. It is estimated that 151 million vaccines are lost due to improper refridgeration, known as the cold-chain problem.

Recognizing this, our team created a design for a vaccine storage device that would ensure that the vaccines maintained cool and protect them from damage to the external environment.

The lare aluminum container on the outside protects from the environment. Inside, we implemented a passive cooling tube, stored with phase change material. This substance has a higher melting point than water, making it longer lasting.

  • Type: Student Project
  • Date: September - December 2015
  • Focus Areas: Global Health
